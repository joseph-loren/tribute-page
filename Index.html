Pass the Lavoisier

A site dedicated to The Father of Modern Chemistry,
Antoine-Laurent de Lavoisier

A simple biographical timeline:
  • 1743 - 26 August, born in Paris, France.
  • 1754 - Lavoisier's formal education begins at the Collège des Quatre-Nations, University of Paris (also known as the Collège Mazarin) in Paris. He studied there until 1761.
  • 1763 - Lavoisier graduates with a bachelor's degree in law and one year later is admitted to the bar.
  • 1764 - Lavoisier reads his first paper to the french Academy of Sciences, covering the chemical and physical properties of gypsum (hydrated calcium sulfate).
  • 1766 - Awarded a gold medal from the king of France for his analysis of urban street lighting problems.
  • 1768 - Provisionally admitted to the Academy of Sciences
  • 1769 - Contributes to the first geological map of France.
  • 1772 - Conducts a study on the reconstruction of fire-damaged Hôtel-Dieu Hospital allowing for clean air filtration.
  • 1772 - Lavoisier's first great contribution to science takes place in the form of a study on combustion. This leads the way in disproving the Phlogistan Theory.
  • 1774 - In his publication Opuscules physiques et chimiques (Physical and Chemical Essays), a conclusion is reached in a study of "fixed air" (CO2) that agrees with Joseph Black.
